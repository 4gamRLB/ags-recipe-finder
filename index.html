AGS Safe Reference Guide
A comprehensive at-a-glance reference covering foods, hidden ingredients, non-food products, and medications. Compiled from AGS research and community knowledge.

Some AGS individuals are also sensitive to shellfish and/or eggs. Always verify with your allergist before using any product you are unsure about.

Meats, Proteins & Organs

Never Safe — Mammal Meats

  • Beef, pork, lamb, veal
  • Venison (deer), bison, elk, boar
  • Rabbit, goat, horse
  • Organs: liver, kidney, heart, intestines, tongue, sweetbreads
  • Rocky Mountain / prairie oysters

Always Safe — Poultry & Seafood

  • Chicken, turkey, duck, goose, quail
  • Emu & ostrich (ratite birds — safe for AGS)
  • All fish (salmon, tuna, cod, tilapia…)
  • All shellfish (shrimp, crab, lobster, clams)*
  • Eggs (chicken, duck, quail)*
Fats, Broths & Flavorings

Avoid

  • Lard (rendered pork fat) — hidden in refried beans, pie crusts, tortillas
  • Tallow (rendered beef fat)
  • Suet (beef or mutton fat)
  • Beef, pork broth / bouillon / stock
  • Gravy (almost always mammal-based)

Use Caution

  • Natural flavorings — may be animal-derived; always check with manufacturer
  • Smoke flavoring — occasionally animal-derived
  • Pre-seasoned meats — may contain mammal broths

Safe Alternatives

  • Coconut oil, avocado oil, olive oil
  • Duck fat, chicken schmaltz
  • Chicken broth, vegetable broth
  • Plant-based bouillon cubes
Thickeners & Stabilizers

Avoid

  • Gelatin — in gummies, marshmallows, Jell-O, puddings, some roasted nuts
  • Collagen — in protein powders, bone broths, supplements

Use Caution

  • Carrageenan — seaweed-derived but may cross-react in highly sensitive individuals

Safe Alternatives

  • Agar-agar (use 1:1 for gelatin)
  • Pectin (for jams, jellies)
  • Arrowroot, cornstarch, tapioca starch
  • Dandies marshmallows (vegan, gelatin-free)
Dairy & Dairy Alternatives

Avoid — Mammal Dairy

  • Cow's milk, goat's milk
  • Butter, cream, sour cream
  • Cheese, yogurt, ice cream
  • Whey & casein (common in protein powders, baked goods)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Lactose

Safe Dairy Alternatives

  • Oat milk, almond milk, soy milk, rice milk
  • Coconut milk & coconut cream
  • Coconut oil or avocado oil (for butter)
  • Vegan / plant-based cheeses
  • Coconut-based yogurt and ice cream
Hidden Additives in Packaged Foods

Check the Source

  • Glycerin / Glycerol — plant-based OR animal-derived; check label
  • Magnesium Stearate — in pills & supplements; occasionally from cow/pig fat
  • Mono- & diglycerides — source varies by manufacturer
  • Rennet — traditional cheese-making uses animal stomach; look for "vegetable rennet"
  • Carmine / cochineal (red dye E120) — insect-derived, debated for AGS
  • "Natural flavors" — always contact manufacturer to verify source
  • Vitamin D3 — often derived from lanolin (sheep's wool)

Always Avoid

  • Bone broth (from any mammal)
  • Animal-derived collagen peptides
  • Lard in canned/packaged foods (check refried beans, pie fillings)
Non-Food Products & Medications

Alpha-gal can be present in non-food items. Always consult your doctor or pharmacist before taking new medications or supplements.

Glycerin in Personal Care

Found in soaps, lotions, lip balms. Can be plant-based or animal-derived. Check with the manufacturer if source is not listed.

Magnesium Stearate in Supplements

Used as a flowing agent in vitamins and medications. Often animal-derived. Ask your pharmacist for plant-based capsule alternatives.

Lanolin in Cosmetics

A wax from sheep's wool. Found in lip balms, diaper rash creams, ointments, and moisturizers. Also hidden as Vitamin D3 in fortified foods.

Medications to Discuss With Your Doctor

Gelatin-coated capsules, heparin, certain antivenoms, and some vaccines may contain gelatin or other mammalian excipients. Always inform your healthcare provider about your AGS diagnosis.

⚠️ This is general awareness information only — not medical advice. Always consult your doctor or pharmacist before changing or stopping any medication.

Quick Substitution Guide

Cooking & Baking Swaps

  • Butter → coconut oil or avocado oil (1:1)
  • Lard → coconut oil or duck fat (1:1)
  • Beef/pork broth → chicken or vegetable broth (1:1)
  • Gelatin → agar-agar (1:1) or pectin
  • Dairy milk → oat, almond, or coconut milk (1:1)
  • Cream → full-fat coconut cream (1:1)
  • Marshmallows → Dandies brand (vegan)
  • Ghee → coconut oil (1:1)
  • Whey protein → plant-based protein powder
  • Cheese → nutritional yeast or vegan cheese
  • Sour cream → coconut cream + lemon juice

Pantry Staples to Keep On Hand

  • Coconut oil & coconut cream
  • Avocado oil & olive oil
  • Vegetable & chicken broth (check labels)
  • Agar-agar powder
  • Arrowroot or cornstarch
  • Plant-based milk (oat, almond, soy)
  • Dandies vegan marshmallows
  • Nutritional yeast (cheese flavor)
