## What Is Alpha-Gal Syndrome?

Alpha-Gal Syndrome (AGS) is an allergic reaction to **galactose-alpha-1,3-galactose (alpha-gal)**, a sugar molecule found in the flesh and byproducts of most mammals. It is typically triggered by a lone star tick bite.

People with AGS must avoid:
- All mammal meats (beef, pork, lamb, venison, etc.)
- Mammal-derived fats (lard, tallow, suet)
- Mammal dairy (milk, butter, cream, cheese)
- Gelatin and rennet
- Many hidden mammal-derived additives in packaged foods

**Always verify with your allergist.** Individual sensitivities vary widely.
